JUNCTION FIELD-EFFECT TRANSISTOR

A JFET is a voltage-controlled tap on a current. Electrons flow through an n-type silicon channel from source to drain. Two p-type gate regions sit on either side of that channel. Drag the controls below: VGS squeezes the channel by growing the depletion region; VDS pushes current through whatever channel is left.

Each curve is one fixed VGS. The dot marks where the device sits right now. Notice the curve flattens (saturates) once VDS is large enough to pinch the channel off at the drain end.

Open channel VGS near 0 V means almost no depletion. The n-type channel is at its widest, so it behaves like a simple resistor — current rises roughly linearly with VDS.
Channel narrowing As VGS goes more negative, the gate–channel junctions reverse-bias further. Their depletion regions swell inward from both sides, leaving a thinner path for electrons.
Pinch-off & saturation Even before the channel fully closes, rising VDS pinches it off near the drain end first. Past that point ID barely increases — the JFET is now acting as a current source set by VGS.
